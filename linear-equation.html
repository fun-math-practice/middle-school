➕➖ Part 2: Multiplying with Negative Numbers Quick Review
When you plug a negative x into y = mx + b, you need to multiply correctly.
Here are the four rules — memorise them! 👇
(+) × (+) = (+)
positive × positive = positive
e.g. 3 × 2 = 6
(+) × (−) = (−)
positive × negative = negative
e.g. 3 × (−2) = −6
(−) × (+) = (−)
negative × positive = negative
e.g. (−3) × 2 = −6
(−) × (−) = (+)
negative × negative = positive
e.g. (−3) × (−2) = 6
💡 Easy trick: Same signs → positive result  |  Different signs → negative result
